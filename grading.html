Mastery-Based Grading System

This system is designed to prioritize learning, iteration, and conceptual understanding over point-chasing.

Levels

Level 3Mastery

You have mastered the material for this assignment.

Demonstrated by

  • Complete understanding of core concepts
  • Accurate application of principles
  • Clear and precise explanations
  • Effective problem-solving strategies
  • Consistently high-quality work

What it means

  • You have met the learning objectives
  • You can transfer ideas to new situations
  • No significant gaps in understanding
Level 2Progressing

You are close, but the work needs refinement.

Demonstrated by

  • Solid understanding with minor gaps
  • Most concepts applied correctly
  • Small errors in execution
  • Good effort with room for improvement

How to upgrade to Level 3

  • Revise based on feedback
  • Submit an improved version within the revision window (see below)
  • Be ready to explain the fixes if asked
Level 1Developing

The work needs significant correction or is incomplete.

Demonstrated by

  • Fundamental misunderstandings
  • Inconsistent application of concepts
  • Multiple significant errors
  • Incomplete or missing parts

Next steps

  • Required: come to office hours (or schedule a short meeting)
  • Revise after consultation
  • Use additional practice resources if assigned
Level 0No submission

Nothing submitted for evaluation.

Nothing can come of nothing.

— William Shakespeare, King Lear, Act I, Scene I

Next steps

  • Start the assignment as soon as possible
  • Come to office hours early (this course is cumulative)

Revision and Late Submission Policy

Programming is cumulative: falling behind makes later topics harder. At the same time, unlimited late work becomes unmanageable. The policy below is designed to balance both.

Standard deadlines

  • Assignments are due on the posted due date/time.
  • Work submitted after the deadline is considered late.

Grace period (automatic)

  • 48-hour grace window: you may submit within 48 hours after the deadline with no penalty.
  • Use this for occasional life surprises; do not plan around it.

Late passes (limited)

  • Each student gets 2 late passes for the semester.
  • A late pass extends the deadline by 7 days (beyond the grace period).
  • To use a late pass, email me before the original deadline with the subject: CS1 Late Pass – [Assignment].

Hard cutoffs

  • No submissions are accepted more than 7 days after the due date unless a late pass is used.
  • No submissions are accepted after we have moved two units past the assignment (or after solutions are discussed in depth in class).
  • End-of-semester cutoff: all work must be submitted by the date posted on the course calendar.

Revisions for Level upgrades

  • If you receive Level 1 or Level 2, you may revise within 1 week of receiving feedback.
  • Revisions must specifically address the feedback; do not simply resubmit the same work.
  • Some revisions may require a brief check-in to confirm understanding.

Why this system benefits you

  • Focus on understanding: you spend time learning, not negotiating points.
  • Growth is rewarded: early mistakes are not permanent.
  • Clear pathway: Level 1 → Level 2 → Level 3 is a structured improvement process.
