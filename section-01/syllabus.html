Course Description

This course provides an introduction to Computer Science and programming, focusing on programming as the foundation for understanding and exploring the world of computers. Students will learn essential programming concepts and develop coding skills through hands-on practice. The course is designed to be flexible and adaptive to the needs of students, whether they are majoring in Mathematics/Computer Science or coming from other disciplines. We will begin with simple programming assignments, gradually building toward more complex tasks. By the end of the semester, students will work on individualized projects that showcase their acquired skills and align with their personal interests or academic focus. Our primary programming language will be Processing, a Java-based language with its own Integrated Development Environment (IDE). Processing is widely recognized for its versatility and accessibility, making it an excellent choice for beginners and those interested in creative coding applications.

Course Goals

This course has two goals: to teach students to "talk to the computers in the computer's own language" and to prepare Math/CS majors for Computer Science 2 and upper-level electives.

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, successful students should be able to

  • Use basic computer terminology
  • Understand Processing syntax including
    • variables and data types
    • conditionals
    • loops
    • arrays
    • strings
  • Learn how to handle images and program simple animations
  • Make the programs interactive
  • Understand the ideas of Object-Oriented programming (classes, methods, basics of inheritance)
  • Do basic debugging

Policy on ChatGPT and Large Language Models (LLMs)

In short: you may use AI tools for curiosity and exploration, but not to generate or write your assignments.

The use of ChatGPT and other large language models is not allowed for writing or coding assignments in this course, unless you receive explicit permission from me in advance. This class is about learning the material by engaging with it directly - struggling, questioning, and building understanding step by step. You should develop problem-solving patterns, understanding error messages, etc. Letting an AI generate your work short-circuits that learning process. Moreover, these tools often produce incorrect or misleading content. Permitted Uses: Exploratory Investigation. You may use LLMs like ChatGPT to explore broader questions, investigate ideas, or find sources—just not to generate or write your actual assignments. For example, questions like “What is the difference between compiling and running a program?”or “What are some visual ways to understand loops or conditionals?” are curiosity-driven and meant to spark learning and discovery, not solve assigned problems for you. If you're unsure whether something is allowed, just ask me—I'm happy to help clarify.

Midterm and Final Projects:

Midterm Project
Students may either build on previous work or develop something entirely new. The project must demonstrate the use of variables, functions, and arrays. Incorporating media—such as images, fonts, sound, and/or video—is encouraged.
Final Project
Students again have two options:

  • Extend the Midterm Project
    The proposal must include a detailed analysis of the planned changes, explaining both what modifications will be made and why they are significant.
  • Start a New Project
    Students may design a project of their choice, applying the skills and tools developed throughout the semester. Possible project types include tools, games, visualizations, animations, interactive artworks, or other software projects.

Presentation and Report

  • Both projects must be presented in class.
  • The final project must be accompanied by a written report. Please consult Brightspace for the final project report guidelines

Exams/Quizzes

The midterm exam will be closed book, closed notes, paper and pencil. Questions will focus on reasoning, tracing, and short code fragments rather than long programs. Expect pop quizzes on current material
